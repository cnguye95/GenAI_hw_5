TEST: Cautious case — scaling sourdough 5x

USER PROMPT
-----------
Scale this sourdough loaf recipe 5x:
500g bread flour, 350g water, 100g starter, 10g salt.

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR
-----------------
- The model parses the recipe and sets scale.mode="factor", factor=5.
- The script runs without error.
- The model presents the scaled recipe AND a strong baking caveat in prose.

EXPECTED VALUES
---------------
- scale_factor:    "5"
- bread flour:     2 1/2 kg        (500g * 5 = 2500g → promotes to 5/2 kg)
- water:           1 3/4 kg        (350g * 5 = 1750g → promotes to 7/4 kg)
- starter:         500 g           (does not reach 1000g → stays in g)
- salt:            50 g

EXPECTED WARNINGS (from script)
-------------------------------
- Salt warning fires (factor 5 > 2.5).
- Reliability warning at the 5x boundary: the script's rule is `factor > 5`,
  so factor == 5 is at the edge; this rule may or may not fire at exactly 5.
  Either way is acceptable per PLAN.md.
- "starter" does NOT match the leavening regex (intentional — the regex
  matches /baking soda|baking powder|yeast/i, and "starter" contains none
  of those substrings). So the leavening warning does NOT fire from the
  script.

WHAT THE MODEL SHOULD ADD IN PROSE
----------------------------------
The model is expected to recognize this is sourdough and add its own caveat:
- Sourdough hydration ratios scale linearly, but fermentation time and dough
  handling do NOT.
- A 5x batch (5kg+ of dough) is not a single loaf — recommend dividing into
  multiple loaves or batches.
- Bulk fermentation in a 5x bowl behaves differently due to thermal mass.

WHAT THE GRADER SHOULD SEE
--------------------------
The numerical scaling is correct, the salt warning is present, AND the model
adds its own thoughtful caution about sourdough specifically — beyond what
the script says. The model should NOT silently trust the script for a 5x
sourdough scale.
