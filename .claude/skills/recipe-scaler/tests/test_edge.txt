TEST: Edge case — pan size change with non-integer egg result

USER PROMPT
-----------
Convert this 9x13 brownie recipe to fit an 8x8 pan:
2 cups sugar, 1 cup butter, 4 eggs, 1 cup cocoa, 1 1/2 cups flour,
1 tsp baking powder, 1/2 tsp salt, 2 tsp vanilla.

EXPECTED BEHAVIOR
-----------------
- The model parses the recipe and sets scale.mode="pan",
  from_pan=[9,13], to_pan=[8,8].
- The script computes factor = (8*8) / (9*13) = 64/117 ≈ 0.547.
- The model presents the scaled recipe AND the bake-time note.

EXPECTED VALUES
---------------
- scale_factor:    "64/117 (~0.5470)"
- sugar:           1 1/8 cup       (2 * 64/117 = 128/117 ≈ 1.094 → rounds to 1 1/8)
- butter:          1/2 cup         (64/117 ≈ 0.547 → rounds to 1/2)
- large eggs:      2               (4 * 64/117 = 256/117 ≈ 2.188 → rounds to 2,
                                    delta ≈ 0.188 ≥ 0.15, so a per-ingredient note is added)
- cocoa:           1/2 cup
- flour:           7/8 cup         (3/2 * 64/117 = 32/39 ≈ 0.821)
- baking powder:   1/2 tsp         (64/117 ≈ 0.547)
- salt:            1/4 tsp         (1/2 * 64/117 = 32/117 ≈ 0.274)
- vanilla:         1 1/8 tsp       (2 * 64/117 = 128/117 ≈ 1.094)

EXPECTED WARNINGS
-----------------
- Egg-rounding warning (because the egg count rounded with delta ≥ 0.15).
- Bake-time adjustment warning (because mode == "pan", always added).
- NO leavening warning (factor < 2.0).
- NO salt warning (factor < 2.5).
- NO reliability warning (0.547 is in the 0.2–5x range).

WHAT THE GRADER SHOULD SEE
--------------------------
The factor displayed as a fraction with a decimal approximation. Eggs go to 2
with a per-ingredient note that the original 4 was scaled to ~2.19. The model
should mention bake time will need adjusting and that a smaller, deeper pan
generally bakes longer.
